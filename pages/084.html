Clarify the issue

Clarify things by asking questions or offering comments related to the problem or issue – 'So what's the actual problem?', 'I think we need to talk about this sooner rather than later.', etc.

Address the problem

Take care to focus on facts and examples when you're addressing the problem.

When tensions arise and people are in conflict, they often take up opposing positions and the objective is to 'win'. To resolve conflict constructively, help those involved to focus on resolving issues rather than reinforcing or supporting their positions!

Listen to the other side

Be respectful by taking responsibility for what's yours, challenging unreasonable behaviours, and addressing any emerging performance matters as necessary.

Manage your way to resolution

Get them involved in coming up with solutions to resolve the conflict – this will help give them ownership and buy-in. What's needed is a genuine commitment to try to sort out the issue.

Agree on possible solutions – get honest commitments, and document agreements. Be sure to follow up on progress and monitor processes and timelines to keep everyone on track.
