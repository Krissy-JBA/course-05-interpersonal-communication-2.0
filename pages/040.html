Choose your medium

Pitch presentations

Pitch presentations are a really common way of communicating to groups of people, and are pretty handy for explaining a new idea, system or vision.

And using tools like PowerPoint can make your presentation vivid and exciting - and even increase buy-in from other people.

A letter or email

Most written correspondence today is email, but the written word is still a very useful, efficient way of communicating, especially when:

  • You need a written record of a discussion or agreement.
  • You don't know the other person â€“ e.g. introductory cover letter.
  • You're in a different location to other people.

A quick phone call

The humble phone is a great tool for quick chats to share ideas or make confirmations - especially when you and the person you need to speak to are in different locations.

But remember your manners! If the conversation's going to take a while, set up a time to call and always ask if the other person is free to talk!

Informal conversation

When you're communicating with someone you know well, or work closely with, an informal chat can be the perfect medium. With both of you at ease who knows what amazing ideas and solutions you could come up with!
