Asking effective questions

You can either ask questions to direct the conversation or to create understanding.

The main types of questions you might find useful are:

Open questions

Exploring opinions and attitudes, or encouraging others to keep talking.

Open questions often start with 'Why?' and 'How?'.

Closed questions

Establishing specific facts, drilling down to definitive responses.

Closed questions get answered with a simple 'yes' or 'no'.

Reflective questions

Giving feedback in question form, to reflect on what's been heard.

Reflective questions start with 'are you saying…' or 'what I hear is…'.

Clarifying questions

Asking for more info to gain better understanding.

Used when someone signals a lack of understanding by saying something like 'I don't follow' or 'could you repeat…?'.
