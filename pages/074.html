Assuming similarities

Sure, as humans we all share the same fundamental needs, but we also have different views based on our values, beliefs and attitudes.

Ethnocentrism

This is when you only use the values and standards of your own culture, to judge someone else's.

Discrimination

This is about 'looking down on people' - i.e. choosing to see differences in race, sex, nationality, age or other characteristics as weaknesses or flaws.

Language and tone

This is when you assume all languages have the same vocabulary, slang or tone (all the parts of communication that can get 'lost in translation').

Nonverbal behaviour

Your culture greatly influences where you look while talking, how you hold your body, your gestures and even your postures.

Cultural shock

This is when you feel anxiety and apprehension when faced with cross cultural communications, rather than accepting, living and working with diversity.

Conflict

The definition of conflict (and ideas about how it should be resolved) vary a lot across different cultures.

Best course of action:

if you’re unsure of the cultural rules that apply – just ask!
